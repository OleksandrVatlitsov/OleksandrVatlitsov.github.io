In Kyiv there is a new borscht recipe

components

  • 1½–2 liters of water;
  • 400–500 g pork or beef on the bone.
  • potatoes – 4 pcs. (average)
  • beets – 2 pcs. (small)
  • carrots – 1 pc.
  • onions – 3 pcs. (average)
  • fresh white cabbage – 300 g
  • tomato paste – 2 tbsp. l.
  • sunflower oil – 4-5 tbsp. l.
  • citric acid - a pinch
  • salt, bay leaf, herbs - to taste.

cooking

  1. First, prepare a decoction of two beets. Peel and cut the root vegetable in half. Fill it with water and boil for 40 minutes until tender. Then continue cooking, adding ½ onion.;
  2. Cut 3-4 potatoes into cubes and half a carrot into strips. Cook them together with beets.
  3. Make a fry. Dice half the bell pepper and the other half of the onion. Fry for 3-5 minutes with a small amount of vegetable oil. Add 2 tbsp. l. tomato paste and simmer for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Let the beet broth cool and chop as desired. And then, along with the frying, send it back to the broth.
  5. When all the vegetables are cooked, add sauerkraut with 200 g of brine. Boil for 2-3 minutes and add salt, pepper and 1-2 tbsp. honey
  6. Leave the finished borscht to steep for 15-20 minutes or place it in the oven at 180 degrees during this time.
  7. It is better to serve the next day to allow it to steep. That's what people say!

Kyiv's Mountains

Kyiv Mountains or Kyiv Hills (Ukrainian: Київські гори, Київські пагорби) is a term used in journalism and literature for hills and highlands around the city of Kyiv that range from approximately 150 to almost 200 meters. The city of Kyiv is located within borders of three orographic regions: Dnieper Upland (Cisdnieper Upland) and Polesian and Dnieper lowlands. The hills are located in northern part of the Kyiv plateau where the Dnieper Upland slopes down towards the Dnieper river valley and the Kyiv Polesia. The highest marks recorded in central and southwestern parts of the city (within the Dnieper Upland, particularly the Kyiv plateau). Hilly landscape is the reason for the unusual depths and two-fold escalators of underground Kyiv Metro (subway) stations in the city center (with the deepest one, Arsenalna, measuring 105.5 metres).

Lysa Hora (Ukrainian: Лиса Гора); ("Bald Mountain" meaning barren mountain, featureless mountain) is a large wooded hill in the Ukrainian capital Kyiv, near the confluence of the Dnipro and Lybid' rivers. The hill is now a nature reserve included in the Kyiv Fortress museum.
